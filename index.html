Design Update for Project Canterbury

  • Date Posted: April 12, 2012
  • Author: Jeri
  • Category: News

Project Canterbury is undergoing a design and structure redesign. Currently the sight is hand-coded and the site organization maintained manually. Moving forward, we are moving the site onto Wordpress, a content management system that will allow for increased flexibility, both for the adding of new content and for uses. This current site is a demo of the style that will be applied to the Wordpress platform, and gestures toward the structure of information that will be implemented in the new site. The site allows for both search and for browsing the archive by categories. A fixed vocabulary will be set up for the "categories," and the categories will be drawn upon for the browsing functions of the site. The move to Wordpress will ease the process of organizing the content as it is uploaded.

The design is minimalist, but gestures toward the older print traditions in which many of the pieces were originally produced. The fonts are standard web-fonts, except in the header and navigation. The main focus of the piece pages is the content itself, and the print style sheet will allow for easy exporting of the content. Wordpress also provides helpful resources for helping promote community and conversation that the current site already gestures toward.

About Project Canterbury

Project Canterbury is a free online archive of out-of-print Anglican texts and related modern documents. It was founded in 1999 and is an all-volunteer effort; it is not affiliated officially with any church body. An advisory board helps to strategize about the site and to plan its further development.
